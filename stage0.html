UW Student Productivity Tools

Topic Description

As a student at UW, I find that there is a clear gap in applications to help with various tasks that students constantly do. Moreover, the ones that do exist tend to not be written with a student first approach, and thus they have poor user experiences and adoption. The problem area is pretty broad, but essentially I want to build a tool to help optimize student life at UW (or potentially more broadly, but I think broadening the scope could take away from the user experience). To make it more concrete, the primary areas of interest to me is building tools that help students succeed in their classes. To do so, I think there are a few more specific areas that can be addressed - the cost of text books (some students just don't buy them putting them at a disadvantage), optimizing course schedules, and forming study groups and other relationships (I think having a solid social life is really important in succeeding academically).

I think this area is particularly important to address at UW, because our competitive major system is not very forgiving to students that have a hard time adjusting to college life. For this reason, I think helping students form those initial relationships, develop good strategies for success in class and providing access to the necessary resources to learn is incredibly important and an area for improvement.

Potential Apps

  • Book Exchange - Students by textbooks every quarter, just to sell them back to the bookstore for half of the money. The idea is pretty simple, input what books you have and the classes you plan to take. The system would match you with students that have the books you need and need the books you have. Hypothetically, this could mean only ever buying books for one quarter.
    Breakdown of costs of college
  • Find Nearest Courses - A lot of students don't go to class because they are too far away (especially in winter quarter). This app would use Google maps and course requirements to find a schedule that optimizes for the least amount of walking. Additionally, this could be helpful for students with mobility related disabilities that have trouble getting to classes on time.
    Students typically cite being too tired as a reason for not going to class. Helping shorten commute to classes (and between them) could help prevent this.
  • Find Students With Similar Schedules - The idea here is pretty simple. Students would input what classes they are taking and it could form groups within those classes. I think this could be especially helpful for freshen taking large introductory courses (think CSE 142) to form study groups and create a smaller environment inside those large classes.
